**Read/Recco (in no particular order):**
- When Breath Becomes Air - Paul Kalanithi 
  - A story from a neurosurgeon who was unfortunately diagnosed with brain cancer. Really just a beautiful piece of someone's life.
- The Hate U Give - Angie Thomas
  - (Warning - Violence) The true story of a black teenager witnessing the shooting of her black friend by a - white police officer, and how she deals with the aftermath. Pretty gripping, and at times even hard to listen to, but I highly recommend.
- Zen and The Art Of Motorcycle Maintenance - Robert M. Pirsig
  - An inquiry into values and the idea of quality. If anyone has a book recommendation that has similar/opposing themes, I'd love to read it. 
- Invisible Women - Caroline Criado-Perez
  - Stats & stories highlighting the data bias in a world where the 6 foot male is the norm for most designs. Great insights into medical, political, and home-life bias that women face. I'd recommend this for anyone, especially men looking to better support/understand the women in their lives & everywhere else.
- Outliers - Malcolm Gladwell
  - Some solid examples of small small changes adding up in a person's life can have a large difference. Did you know that most NHL players are born in January, since the peewee draft being in December allows for them to be the largest and therefore fastest?
- Still Standing - Kevin Rempel
  - This book isn't very well known, but is a story of perseverance from the life of a Canadian athlete who was paralyzed (along with his father), and then regained his ability to walk.
- The Life-Changing Magic of Not Giving a F*ck - Sarah Knight
  - A book about saying no and not feeling bad for it! There's a couple of these out there, but I personally enjoyed how the author read this as an audiobook.
- An Astronaut's Guide To Life On Earth - Chris Hadfield
  - Stories from the Canadian commander of the ISS, and what it takes to become an astronaut.
- The Signal and the Noise - Nate Silver
  - Interesting stories of stats and predictions, and the importance of uncertainty. I'd recommend this for folks with less of a stats background who want to better understand some of stats behind models/how to communicate findings.
